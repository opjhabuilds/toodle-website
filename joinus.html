My life, like yours, is a constant juggle. Work deadlines. Family commitments. Personal ambitions. Daily chores. Travel plans. Health goals. Finances. Taking care of parents. Keeping up with the kids. That leaking tap you keep saying you'll fix. That call you keep meaning to make.

The list never ends. Some days I feel on top of everything. Most days, something important slips through the cracks.

A doctor's appointment I forgot to book. A birthday gift I meant to buy. A work task I was sure I'd remember. A promise I made to my family that got buried under everything else.

The worst part? It wasn't that I didn't care. I cared deeply. I just couldn't keep track of it all.

I tried everything.

Todoist. Notion. Google Tasks. Notes apps. Paper planners. Sticky notes on my desk. Reminders in my phone. I even tried building elaborate spreadsheets.

Some were too complex, built for project managers running corporate sprints, not for a regular person managing a real life. Some were too basic, just a blank page with no structure to help me prioritise what actually matters today. And none of them made me feel good about getting things done. They just felt like more work on top of work.

"I wanted something in between. Something that understood that my life isn't a project to be managed. It's a day to be lived well."

So I built it. I called it Toodle.

Toodle is the to-do app I wished existed. It has a simple priority system (P0 for critical, P1 for important, P2 for normal) so I always know what to tackle first. It has themes so I can separate Work from Home from Health from anything else. And it has this small, surprisingly motivating thing: you earn stars for completing your daily tasks. Up to 5 stars a day.

I know it sounds small. But on the days I hit 5 stars, I genuinely feel like I won the day. That feeling compounds.

Then my friends tried it.

I shared it with a few close friends. A couple managing a new baby and both working full time. A founder running a small team. A student preparing for competitive exams. A friend who just wanted to stop forgetting groceries.

They all said the same thing: "Why doesn't something this simple already exist?"

That's when I realised this might be useful to more people than just me.

And that brings us to you.

If you've landed on this page, it's because someone in your life shared Toodle with you. They thought it could help. That's a small act of care, someone wanting you to have a better, more organised day.

Toodle is in beta right now. It's not perfect. There are features I'm still building, bugs I'm still squashing, and ideas I haven't even started on. But the core experience (adding tasks, prioritising them, swiping through your day, and earning your stars) that works. And it works well.
